# Translation
**Status:** Frontier

Neural machine translation has reached near-human quality for many language pairs and content types. Business documents, technical manuals, and general content can be translated automatically with minimal editing. Literary translation, cultural adaptation, and specialized domains still need human translators.

## Latest
- **2026-07-16**: A Chinese university plans to phase out compulsory English classes in favor of intercultural communication, citing real-time translation tools as part of the change. This is an early institutional signal that routine language transfer is becoming less scarce, while cultural understanding and independent judgment remain human advantages. Source: [South China Morning Post](https://www.scmp.com/news/china/science/article/3360516/will-ai-kill-english-classes-chinese-university-drop-them-tech-booms)

## History
- **2025-11** -- Specialized and creative translation remains human; general translation is largely automated. Source: Slator
- **2025-04** -- Translation rates drop as turnaround expectations shift from days to hours. Source: Industry data
- **2024-09** -- Professional translation agencies adopt "post-editing" model: AI translates, humans refine. Source: Slator
- **2024-03** -- Real-time voice translation launches in consumer devices (Google Pixel, Apple). Source: Tech press
- **2023-01** -- DeepL and Google Translate improve significantly with LLM-based approaches. Source: DeepL
