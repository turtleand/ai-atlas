# Basic Prompt Engineering
**Status:** Frontier

As models get smarter, the need for elaborate prompting techniques decreases. Simple tricks like few-shot examples and chain-of-thought are being absorbed into model capabilities. The models figure out what you mean without careful prompt construction.

## Latest
- **2026-02-24** -- Reports surface that major AI providers are quietly degrading model output quality to manage compute costs, reducing context windows and simplifying reasoning chains. Users document measurable regressions in flagship models. For prompt engineers, this means carefully crafted prompts may produce inconsistent results across model versions, further eroding the value of static prompt optimization as a skill. Source: Developer forums, Industry analysis

## History
- **2026-01** -- Advanced prompt engineering evolves into system design and evaluation. Source: Industry consensus
- **2025-08** -- Basic prompting knowledge becomes a general skill, not a specialized role. Source: Industry reports
- **2025-01** -- Models with built-in reasoning (o1, Claude thinking) reduce need for chain-of-thought prompting. Source: Anthropic, OpenAI
- **2024-07** -- Claude and GPT-4 handle complex tasks with minimal prompting. Source: Anthropic
- **2024-01** -- "Prompt engineer" job postings peak, then begin declining. Source: Industry data
