# Complex Problem Solving

Complex problem solving remains above the waterline because the durable work is framing the problem, choosing the proof target, judging assumptions, and taking responsibility for consequences. AI can compress formal substeps, but it does not decide what should be proven or why the proof matters.

This note records Turtleand's interpreted signals from public news sources. It is not a universal disruption score.

## Latest

- **2026-07-01**: Mistral's Leanstral 1.5 model for Lean 4 proof work shows AI moving deeper into formal reasoning and automated theorem proving. The pressure rises on routine proof assistance, while the human edge moves toward problem selection, specification quality, verification goals, and judgment under uncertainty. Source: [Mistral AI docs](https://docs.mistral.ai/models/model-cards/leanstral-1-5-26-06)

## History

- **2026-02**: Earlier reasoning-model progress suggested that complex work would be decomposed into machine-assisted subproblems, but humans still had to define the objective, inspect the reasoning chain, and accept the risk of action.
