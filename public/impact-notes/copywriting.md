# Copywriting
**Status:** Submerged (changed 2026-02-21)

AI can write competent marketing copy, email campaigns, social media posts, and ad variations. The quality is good enough for many use cases. But brand voice, emotional resonance, and truly creative campaigns still need human writers.

## Latest
- **2026-02-26** -- Paligo research analyzing 74.3 million ChatGPT prompt uses reveals article and blog writing dominates all AI automation with 35.3 million uses -- 47.4% of total demand. The single most-used prompt (1.9M uses) is a "humanized, SEO-optimized, long-form article writer." Creative writing hit 1.4M uses, marketing copy 1.4M, and call-to-action text 1.2M. Content production has gone industrial-scale through LLMs, with writing-related tasks appearing repeatedly in the top 10 across all categories. Meanwhile, editing/proofreading templates only attracted 360K uses, suggesting workers skip dedicated tools and paste directly into chat. Source: [PPC Land](https://ppc.land/workers-bet-on-ai-to-write-articles-but-the-data-shows-cracks/)
- **2026-02-24** -- European publisher Mediahuis (25 titles including De Telegraaf, Irish Independent) is testing a multi-agent AI pipeline to automate routine news production. Separate AI agents handle commissioning, writing, fact-checking, legal review, and image selection. A human editor reviews before publishing. Head of AI strategy Ana Jakimovska says the goal is to free 2,000 journalists for "signature journalism" -- talking to people, knocking on doors. A monitoring agent also tracks post-publication audience discourse and flags topics needing deeper reporting. Source: [Media Copilot](https://mediacopilot.ai/mediahuis-ai-agents-first-line-news/)

## History
- **2026-02-19** -- Analysis of 8,000 U.S. content marketing job listings shows writing is being replaced by "content creation" as the primary skill. Mid-level generalist titles dropped 70%+ since 2023, while execution-heavy roles now make up 34% of listings and senior ownership roles (Head/VP of Content) grew 300-375%. AI is now a baseline expectation, appearing in 34% of senior and 19% of non-senior roles, though specific AI skills (prompt engineering) appear in less than 1% of listings. Companies want content marketers to own visibility across search, AI-driven discovery, and storytelling, not just write blog posts. Source: [Semrush](https://www.semrush.com/blog/content-marketing-job-market-study/)
- **2026-02-18** -- Senate Democrats project AI could displace 100 million U.S. jobs over the next decade, including content creators and marketing professionals. The report comes as reinforcement learning becomes a $17B industry, with experts teaching AI to master their own professions. Source: [InvestorPlace](https://investorplace.com/2026/02/danger-lurking-behind-strong-gdp-number/)
